# How to Open Browser Console

## Safari (macOS):
1. **Enable Developer Menu first** (one-time setup):
   - Go to Safari → Settings (or Preferences)
   - Click on "Advanced" tab
   - Check "Show features for web developers"

2. **Open Console**:
   - Press `Cmd+Option+C` 
   - OR right-click on the page → "Inspect Element" → Click "Console" tab
   - OR Safari menu → Develop → Show JavaScript Console

## Chrome/Edge:
1. Press `F12` or `Cmd+Option+I` (Mac) / `Ctrl+Shift+I` (Windows)
2. Click the "Console" tab at the top

## Firefox:
1. Press `F12` or `Cmd+Option+I` (Mac) / `Ctrl+Shift+I` (Windows)
2. Click the "Console" tab

## Quick Test:
Once the console is open, you should see:
- Any errors in red
- Console.log messages (like "Playing playlist:" or "No active player")
- Network requests

Try clicking the play/shuffle buttons and watch the console for messages!
